Stacey Dash | Bio, Age, Net Worth, Relationship, Career

Stacey Dash (born Stacey Lauretta Dash) is an American actress and former talk show presenter. Dash is most remembered for her role as Dionne Marie Davenport in the 1995 film Clueless and its related television series.

Stacey’s other credits include Moving, Mo’ Money, Renaissance Man, and View from the Top. Here are a few fun facts about her. Keep reading to find out more about her.

Stacey Dash was born in The Bronx, New York City, America. Her father’s name is Dennish Dash, and her mother’s name is Linda Dash. Is Stacey Dash Single? Relationship

According to her personal life, Stacey is a married lady. She has been married four times and divorced three times in her life. She married producer Brian Lovell in 1999. The couple split in the mid-2000s.

Lola is their child. Dash then married British entrepreneur James Maby, the CEO of Sports Logistics, from 2005 to 2006. She later married actor Emmanuel Xuereb in 2009, filed for divorce in January 2010, and divorced in 2011.

Dash was once again associated with vocalist Christopher Williams. Their first child, Austin, was born in 1991. On April 6, 2018, she married attorney Jeffrey Marty in Florida. She admitted to meeting Marty 10 days before the wedding. Dash, in addition to her own two children, is the stepmother to three of Marty’s children.

How did Dash start her Professional Career?

Stacey made her television debut in the 1982 NBC crime drama pilot Farrell: For the People, which featured Valerie Harper and Ed O’Neill but did not make it past the pilot episode. Dash appeared as a femme fatale in the low-budget film Illegal in Blue in 1995. Clueless, an adolescent comedy film released in 1995, gave her her big break.

Dash repeated her role as Dionne in the 1966 television spin-off of the same name. The show aired from 1996 until 1999. She appeared in View from the Top after the TV show had ended in 2003. Her lingerie collection, Letters of Marque, launched in 2006.

She completed I Could Never Be Your Woman, Nora’s Hair Salon II, Fashion Victim, Ghost Image, and American Primitive in 2007. In 2008, Dash appeared in the reality show Celebrity Circus. She appeared as a recurrent character on the television show The Game in early 2009.

Dash, on the other side, faced criticism for claiming that the BET Awards misled black people about the Oscar boycott due to a lack of ethnic diversity, and she advocated for the abolition of Black History Month in 2016. Dash’s contract with Fox News expired on January 21, 2017.
