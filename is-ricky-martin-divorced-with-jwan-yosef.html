Is Ricky Martin Divorced with Jwan Yosef?

Ricky Martin, the Puerto Rican singer and actor who is widely regarded as the “King of Latin Pop”, has been married to Jwan Yosef, a Syrian-Swedish artist, since 2017.

However, in July 2023, the couple announced that they were divorcing after six years of marriage.

They said in a joint statement that they decided to end their marriage with love, respect, and dignity for their children and honor what they have experienced as a couple.

They also expressed their desire to continue having a healthy family dynamic and a relationship centered on peace and friendship to continue the joint upbringing of their four children.

Ricky Martin  career

Ricky Martin began his career at the age of 12, as a member of the Puerto Rican boy band Menudo.

He left the group in 1989 and pursued a solo career, releasing his first album in 1991.

He gained recognition in Latin America and Europe with his albums Me Amaras (1993) and A Medio Vivir (1995), which featured his first international hit “María”.

His breakthrough came with his fourth album Vuelve (1998), which included the songs “Vuelve” and “La Copa de la Vida”, the latter being the official theme of the 1998 FIFA World Cup.

He performed “La Copa de la Vida” at the 41st Grammy Awards, which was widely praised as a game-changer for Latin music.

His first English album, Ricky Martin (1999), became his first US Billboard 200 number-one and sold over 15 million copies worldwide.

The lead single “Livin’ la Vida Loca” topped both the Billboard Hot 100 and the UK Singles Chart.

Martin’s success in the late 1990s is generally seen as the beginning of the “Latin explosion”, which opened the doors for many other Latin artists to achieve global fame.

He has since released several more albums in both Spanish and English, such as Almas del Silencio (2003), MTV Unplugged (2006), A Quien Quiera Escuchar (2015), and Pausa (2020).

Some of his most popular singles include “She Bangs”, “Nobody Wants to Be Lonely”, “Tal Vez”, “Tu Recuerdo”, “La Mordidita”, “Vente Pa’ Ca”, and “Canción Bonita”.
