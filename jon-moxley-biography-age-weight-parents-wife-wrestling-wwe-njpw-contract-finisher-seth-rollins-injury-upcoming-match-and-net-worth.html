Jon Moxley Biography

Ohio born wrestler and actor, Jon Moxley ( Jonathan David Good) is well known as a professional wrestler currently signed to All Elite Wrestling (AEW), where he performs. He also makes appearances in New Japan Pro-Wrestling (NJPW).

Jon Moxley Wrestler

Since his young age, Moxley developed an interest in wrestling at a very tender age. He revealed named Bret Hart as his childhood idol He used wrestling to escape his rough upbringing by watching video wrestling and reading stories about wrestling in the early days.

A year after he started training as a wrestler, he dropped out of high school. He funded himself by working in restaurants and factories, but he was regularly fired because he would skip work if he had been scheduled to fight a match that had interfered with his shift.

He started working for Les Thatcher in the Heartland Wrestling Association (HWA) as a teenager by selling popcorn and setting up a ring. At the age of 18, he began training as a professional wrestler under Thatcher and Cody Hawk. He made his debut with the ring name Jon Moxley in June 2004, losing his debut match against Ala Hussein.

The next year, Moxley won the HWA Tag Team Championship twice with Jimmy Turner and Ric Byrne. He captured the HWA Heavyweight Championship twice in 2006 by beating Pepper Parks on both occasions but losing the belt to Chad Collyer and Brian Jennings.

On May 25, 2019, Moxley made his unrecognized debut for All Elite Wrestling (AEW) at their Double or Nothing match, assaulting Chris Jericho, Kenny Omega, and the referee during Jericho and Omega’s main event. Shortly thereafter, it was revealed that Moxley had signed a multi-year extension.

Jon Moxley All Elite Wrestling

On May 1, Good posted a video on Twitter advertising the return of his gimmick to Jon Moxley. On May 25, Moxley made his unrecognized debut for All Elite Wrestling (AEW) at their Double or Nothing match, targeting Chris Jericho, Kenny Omega, and the referee during Jericho and Omega’s main event. Soon thereafter, it was revealed that Moxley had signed a multi-year deal.

In June, Moxley had his AEW debut match at the Fyter Fest pay-per-view, where he beat Joey Janela in an unsanctioned match. After the match, Moxley was assaulted by Omega in retaliation for his previous assault. The match between the two was scheduled for All-Out on August 31st.

Nevertheless, on August 23, Moxley was forced out of the event after being diagnosed with MRSA staph infection in his elbow and undergoing surgery to remove it. Subsequently, AEW announced Pac as the substitution of Moxley at All Out.

Jon Moxley NJPW

While AEW is Moxley’s “home base” promotion, he has been a key figure in New Japan since he made his company debut in June. Moxley beat Juice Robinson for the U.S. IWGP championship in his first match and later entered the G1 Climax tournament.

Following weeks of New Japan’s Pro-Wrestling (NJPW) airing mystery man videos threatening IWGP United States Champion Juice Robinson, Moxley was found guilty in May 2019, challenging Robinson for a title match on the final night of the Best of the Super Juniors 26 show on June 5.

In the event, he defeated Robinson in his NJPW in-ring debut to win the IWGP United States Championship. Despite Moxley’s original challenge, AEW CEO Tony Khan announced that Moxley will be able to make independent and international bookings during the summer before AEW’s TV deal begins in the fall.
