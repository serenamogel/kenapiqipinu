Taeyeon Biography

Born on March 9, 1989, in Jeonju, North Jeolla, South Korea.

She soon began her training and joined the SM Academy, a prestigious school. For three years, she was trained by well-known Korean vocalist Jeong Soon-won (The One).

Soon after graduating from Jeonju Art High School in 2008, Taeyeon began her singing career. 2009 saw the publication of “Girls’ Generation” and her first song, “Gee.”

She has also participated in other projects by SM Entertainment, such as Girls’ Generation-TTS, SM the Ballad, and Girls’ Generation-Oh!GG, and the supergroup Got the Beat.

In 2015, Taeyeon released her debut EP, “I,” which featured the hit single of the same name that topped South Korea’s Gaon Digital Chart. She followed this with the number-one single “Rain” as part of the SM Station project, and her second EP, “Why,” which reached the top spot on the Gaon Album Chart.

Her debut studio album, “My Voice,” was released in 2017 and featured the successful singles “11:11,” “Fine,” and “Make Me Love You.”

Taeyeon also ventured into the Japanese music scene with the digital single “Stay” in 2018 and the EP “Voice” in 2019.

Her second studio album, “Purpose,” was released in 2019 and included the popular singles “Four Seasons” and “Spark.” Her third studio album, “INVU,” was released in 2022 and was preceded by the top-10 single “Can’t Control Myself.”

She has appeared on various television shows, including We Got Married (2009), Begin Again (2019), and as a host on Queendom 2 (2022). She is currently a cast member of Amazing Saturday (2020-present). Additionally, she has recorded soundtracks for several television dramas and films, including “If” for Hong Gil-dong (2008), “Can You Hear Me” for Beethoven Virus (2008), and “All about You,” which topped the charts for Hotel del Luna (2019).
