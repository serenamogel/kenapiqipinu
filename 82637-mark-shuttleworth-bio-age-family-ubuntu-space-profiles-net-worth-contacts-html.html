Mark Shuttleworth education

Where did Mark Shuttleworth go to school? He started his nursery school at Western Province Preparatory School. Later, he proceeded to Rondebosch Boys High School, where he only schooled for one term before shifting to Bishops Diocesan College. While there, he was the Head Boy, a position he held due to his outstanding performance and discipline.

After his high school education, he developed an interest in information technology and that saw him enroll at the University of Cape Town to pursue a Bachelor of Business Science degree in Finance and Information Systems.

Mark Shuttleworth career

While at the university, he took part in the first residential Internet connections. That prompted him to establish Thawte, an IT consulting firm that later became a world leader in Internet security for electronic commerce. Four years down the line, he sold the company to VeriSign, a US-based company. With the proceeds, he founded a venture capital firm alongside a non-profit organization that was dedicated to funding education programs in the African continent.

Mark Shuttleworth ubuntu

In 2004, he started the development of Ubuntu through his company Canonical Ltd. This is a free and open-source Linux distribution based on Debian. The company fueled the modern world and created an operating system Ubuntu, which can connect almost everything to the internet.

Canonical Limited

In 2004, he founded Canonical Limited. This is a computer software company created for promotion and market commercial support. He became the company CEO from 2004 to 2010, when he stepped down for Jane Silber to take over. However, he was to later reclaim the position in 2017, a position he holds to date.

Shuttleworth Foundation

Mark, being a philanthropist, started a nonprofit-making organization in 2001, Shuttleworth Foundation. It looks out for social innovators who are helping to change the world for the better.
